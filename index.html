Research

Direct Air Capture: Mg-Carbonate Flame Retardant

Objective
Developed and evaluated a sustainable flame retardant derived from direct air capture (DAC) technology by incorporating magnesium carbonate into polymer composites and assessing its fire performance and thermal properties.
Result
Produced and characterized DAC-derived magnesium carbonate, fabricated polymer composites with varying additive concentrations, and analyzed their fire resistance and thermal behavior through standardized flammability and material characterization tests. The project provided insight into the feasibility of DAC-derived magnesium carbonate as an environmentally friendly flame retardant.

Plant Design · Nuclear & Process Design

Thorium-Based Nuclear Power Plant: Process Design

Objective
Designed a conceptual 250 MW thorium molten salt nuclear power plant by integrating process simulation, utility systems, and techno-economic analysis to evaluate its technical and economic feasibility for electricity generation.
Result
Developed an integrated plant design including the reactor system, supercritical CO₂ Brayton power cycle, utility facilities, and supporting process units. Completed mass and energy balances, major equipment sizing, economic evaluation, and profitability analysis to assess the project's overall feasibility.

Process Engineer · Performance Evaluation

Ammonia Refrigeration Compressor Performance Evaluation

Objective
Evaluated the performance of an industrial ammonia refrigeration compressor by comparing its operating conditions with design specifications to assess efficiency, reliability, and potential performance degradation.
Result
Analyzed historical operating data, estimated compressor performance parameters, and quantified the gap between actual and design performance. The evaluation identified efficiency degradation consistent with long-term plant operation and provided engineering insights into factors affecting compressor performance.
